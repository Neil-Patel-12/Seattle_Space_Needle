When and Why was it built?

The construction started April 17,1961 and completed December 1961. It was originally built for the World’s Fair in 1962: Century 21 Exposition with theme “The Age of Space”. The 1960s was the space race, which led the first astronauts landing on the moon. The structure symbolized the humanity’s Space Age aspirations.

Was it built using local materials?

Material provided by the United States, the massive base was made of cement that equated to 467 cement trucks. The legs of the Space Needle consist of three 36-inch-wide flanged beams welded together flange-to-flange and stiffened with diaphragms to form large three-sided tubes. Recently, in 2017, glass and steel have been added to increase strength and functionality.

How has it been used in the past? Always for its intended purpose or has it been adapted for reuse by later generations?

The Space Needle was originally intended for the 21 Century Exposition – Seattle’s World Fair, also sending a message to the world that our future is in technology and space. Now, It houses two restaurant, a radio station and offers 360- degree views from its three main viewing areas: an indoor observation deck, open air viewing area, and a level featuring a revolving glass floor called “The Loupe” at 500 feet above ground.
